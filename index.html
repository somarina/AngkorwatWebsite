About Angkor Wat

Angkor Wat is located in Siem Reap, Cambodia. Built in the early 12th century by King Suryavarman II, it was originally dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu. Later, it became a Buddhist temple and stands today as a national symbol of Cambodia.

Known for its majestic towers, intricate carvings, and symmetry, Angkor Wat represents the peak of Khmer architecture and spiritual devotion. It attracts millions of tourists and remains a place of worship and pride for Cambodians.

The Best Experiences With Angkor Wat Temple

Watch the Sunrise

Experience the breathtaking sunrise over Angkor Wat, one of the most iconic views in Cambodia. Arrive early to secure the best spot by the reflection pond.

Try Khmer Cuisine

Sample authentic Cambodian dishes like Fish Amok, Beef Lok Lak, and Khmer Red Curry at local restaurants near the temple complex.

Explore Bayon Temple

Visit the famous Bayon Temple with its iconic stone faces. Located in Angkor Thom, it's just a short distance from Angkor Wat.

Visit Local Markets

Explore Siem Reap's vibrant markets like Psar Chaa (Old Market) for souvenirs, silk scarves, spices, and local handicrafts.

Watch Apsara Dance

Enjoy a traditional Cambodian Apsara dance performance, often accompanied by dinner at restaurants in Siem Reap.

Visit Tonle Sap Lake

Take a boat tour on Southeast Asia's largest freshwater lake to see floating villages and experience local life on the water.

Location of Angkor Wat Temple

Angkor Wat Temple is situated in the northwestern part of Cambodia, in Siem Reap Province. It is located about 5.5 kilometers north of Siem Reap city center. The temple is easily accessible by road, making it a convenient destination for tourists exploring the region.

Quick Tip: Visit early in the morning to experience the world-famous sunrise view from across the reflection pond.

Travel Information: Reaching Angkor Wat is easy by tuk-tuk, motorbike, or car from Siem Reap town. Visitors need an Angkor Pass available at the main entrance checkpoint.
